We aim to ensure that our customers can complete any online transaction the first time unaided. It is important that any online forms are high quality, accessible, easy to use and built around customer needs. They should only ask for information required to successfully complete the transaction and not gather superfluous information.

Design Patterns

The general design of form elements should follow the GOV.UK Form Elements guidance while adapting to the Oxford City Council style template.

The design of individual form questions (e.g. date fields, addresses) should follow the GOV.UK Design Patterns as these have been thoroughly tested with users to achieve consistently good results.

Although not definitive, we would also suggest developers make use of the Design Patterns hackpad to draw on for their solutions as this is continually being added to.

Web Forms Design Guidance

In 2014 we asked Mando to help us improve our online forms. They tested each one and also undertook user testing on a representative sample. The outcomes of this were captured in a guidance document to assist us and developers that work with us to create great digital services.

Download the guidance document

Although the guidance document represented the best available advice at the time, we're aware that it can now contradict the GOV.UK Design Patterns in places. An example is section 2.9 relating to progress indicators. In these cases, the GOV.UK advice takes priority over our guidance document.

If you're unsure, check with the Digital Development Team for advice.
